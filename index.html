Ice dynamics of Greelandic tidewater glaciers at large and small spatio-temporal scales – with Prof James Lea at the University of Liverpool in September 2022

I hold a Master of Research in Risk and Decisions Making from the Univeristy of Liverpool as well as a Master of Science in Geological and Environmental Hazards from the University of Portsmouth.

My background is in Geoscience, but I had a focus on volcanology and rock mechanics during my undergraduate and first postgraduate degree.

Projects

At the Geological Survey of Denmark and Greenland, I am part of the Center for Ice Sheet and Sea Level Rise Predictions (CISP) and the Semper Crevasses Projects.

Within CISP, my task is to investigate ice-ocean interactions at Petermann Glacier in Northwest Greenland using satellite remote sensing and in-situ observations.
We will install 10 GEUS designed and built GPS stations at Petermann Glacier in summer 2025 to gain further insights into stress/strain across the grounding zone, and on the floating tongue to investigate basal melting.

The Semper Crevasses project investigates a Mega crevasse field (up to 20 m wide) in the accumulation zone of the Greenland Ice Sheet
In this project, I am using Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) data to investigate the evolution of the crevasse field over time, and to evaluate potential causes for the crevasses to open.

Previously I completed a Postdoctoral Resarch Fellowship at the University of Oregon in the Greenland Ice Sheet Ocean Science Network (GRISO).
Within GRISO, I was part of the Ocean Forcing Ice Working Group and worked on quantifying frontal ablation (calving + submarine melting + subaerial melting) from observational data.

The processing chain to quantify frontal ablation and resulting dataset are currently in review, but can be accessed via Zenodo
